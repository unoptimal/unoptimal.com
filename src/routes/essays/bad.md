---
title: Most things are bad, and that's okay
date: "October 17, 2022"
---

People are afraid to make things because [they fear it will be bad](http://www.paulgraham.com/early.html#:~:text=Most%20people%20don%27t%20even,much%20more%20we%27d%20do.).

But most things are bad. In fact, [ninety-percent of things are bad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sturgeon%27s_law).

So you should actually just make the bad thing. Doing so puts you in the 99th percentile of all people (they are not making anything).

But don’t just take my word for it; listen to the rallying call from creators and digital nomads all across the Internet:

- [Ira Glass on Storytelling](https://vimeo.com/24715531)
- [DONE IS BETTER THAN PERFECT](https://valeriezhang.substack.com/p/done-is-better-than-perfect)
- [The explicit point of a first draft is to get you to a second draft](https://www.reddit.com/r/slatestarcodex/comments/axxpdf/comment/ehx6541/)
- [Obvious to you. Amazing to others.](https://sive.rs/obvious)
- [Ideas are cheap. Execution is expensive.](https://twitter.com/ShaneAParrish/status/1453379411504504835)

[Talkers talk; builders build.](https://twitter.com/SahilBloom/status/1490455993553199108) And that’s the separator.
